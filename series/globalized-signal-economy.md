# Globalized Signal Economy

This series examines work, hiring, competence, trust, and institutional selection in a labor market shaped by credential inflation, global competition, AI, outsourcing, weakened work ladders, and attention scarcity.

## Essays

1. [Making Competence Inspectable](../articles/making-competence-inspectable.md)

## Core question

How does real competence become visible when the systems responsible for recognizing it are mediated, risk-averse, globalized, attention-constrained, and increasingly flooded by generic signals?

## Recurring themes

- Cybersecurity hiring as a shortage paradox
- The decline of older middle-class work ladders
- Education as a weaker participation ticket
- Offshoring, remote labor, and global signal competition
- AI and the cheapening of generic content
- Work samples as trust-bearing signals
- Networks, social proof, and prior-title legitimacy
- The difference between ritual and asset
