## Purpose

This document explains the first public field note as a reviewer-facing artifact.

The artifact translates a recurring cybersecurity career argument into a broader selection-environment framework:

```text
credential inflation + degraded work ladders + global labor competition + hiring risk -> need for testable public proof
```

## Review path

### 1. Honest uncertainty

The essay does not present portfolio work as a proven solution. It treats "make competence inspectable" as a hypothesis that needs scrutiny from hiring managers, recruiters, technical reviewers, and candidates.

**Reviewer takeaway:** The artifact avoids false certainty and frames public proof as a testable strategy.

### 2. Self-referential field test

The essay is itself part of the inquiry. It is not interview prep and not a claim that writing proves employability. It attempts to define what public proof is supposed to recover when hiring systems lose direct contact with human judgment.

**Reviewer takeaway:** The artifact demonstrates the exact behavior it analyzes: turning private reasoning into a public, inspectable object.

### 3. Labor-market backdrop

The essay starts with the recurring cybersecurity advice that certifications alone do not get candidates hired. It then broadens the frame to older work-ladder degradation, service-work pressure, globalized competition, and hiring mediation.

**Reviewer takeaway:** The artifact is not generic career advice. It is a labor-market field note tied to cybersecurity hiring.

### 4. Cybersecurity demand versus entry friction

The essay separates occupational growth from entry-level conversion. A field can grow while the candidate-selection interface remains crowded, risk-averse, or difficult for career changers to cross.

**Reviewer takeaway:** The artifact shows practical distinction between macro demand and hiring-layer mechanics.

### 5. Hiring as a selection environment

The essay maps different hiring layers to different signals:

```text
ATS -> keywords and requirements
recruiter -> fit and low-risk presentation
hiring manager -> ramp speed and reduced burden
technical reviewer -> evidence and judgment
senior practitioner -> real understanding of the work
```

**Reviewer takeaway:** The candidate understands that different audiences require different proof.

### 6. Inspectable proof as risk reduction

The essay argues that hands-on artifacts matter only if they can show investigation logic, evidence handling, false-positive reasoning, escalation judgment, documentation quality, and role-specific usefulness.

**Reviewer takeaway:** Portfolio work is framed as reviewer uncertainty reduction, not self-promotion.
