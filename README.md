## What this is

This repository is the canonical Markdown home for a single long-form essay.

It is not a lab repo, a documentation tree, a course archive, or a technical proof map. It is a publication page: one finished essay, one Spanish companion edition, one source map, and one social excerpt.

The broader topic is not only cybersecurity hiring. Cybersecurity is the case study. The deeper subject is how competence becomes visible after older institutions that formed and recognized competence have thinned.

The essay asks why a field can report persistent cybersecurity talent shortages while capable candidates still experience the entry interface as brutal, opaque, and difficult to cross.

The working frame:

```text
A credential says I studied.
A resume says I claim.
A referral says someone trusts me.
An interview says I can perform under evaluation.
A serious work sample says: here is how I think when evidence is incomplete and the decision still matters.
```

That does not solve the labor market.

It gives trust something real to inspect.

## Why a Spanish edition exists

The essay is especially relevant to immigrants, children of immigrants, and first-generation Americans who inherited the belief that education, work ethic, and institutional loyalty would create a reliable bridge into middle-class life.

The Spanish edition is not a secondary summary. It is a full companion translation written in a Northern Mexico / U.S.-Mexico border register: clear Mexican Spanish, closer to Sonora, Baja California, Mexicali, and Nuevo Leon than to academic Spain or neutral corporate translation.

It is available for readers who think through these questions in Spanish, English, or both.
