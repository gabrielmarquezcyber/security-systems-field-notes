# Selection Environment Proof Map

## Core Idea

Cybersecurity hiring is not competence-based by default. It is selection-environment-based.

Different layers select for different signals.

The working prescription is not simply:

```text
Make competence inspectable.
```

The stronger formulation is:

```text
Convert competence into trust-bearing signals.
```

Inspectable proof is one signal. It must be paired with visibility, translation, distribution, social proof, and role-specific relevance.

## Selection Layers

| Layer | What it selects for | Candidate mistake | Better signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| ATS / HR | Keywords, titles, authorization, salary, obvious fit | Assuming skill will be inferred | Resume alignment, role keywords, clean titles, clear eligibility |
| Recruiter | Fit, risk reduction, communication, presentability | Overloading technical detail | Short proof summary, clear story, confidence without overclaiming |
| Hiring Manager | Operational usefulness, ramp speed, workload reduction | Talking in concepts | Role-specific project proof and workflow evidence |
| Technical Reviewer | Evidence, judgment, tool fluency, troubleshooting | Showing only credential logos | Investigation notes, detections, playbooks, queries, false-positive reasoning |
| Team / Client | Trust, documentation, escalation, communication | Treating security as solo puzzle solving | Clear handoffs, timelines, decision notes, client-safe language |
| Network | Familiarity, sponsorship, reputational transfer | Staying invisible | Referrals, professional engagement, useful comments, direct outreach |
| Platform | Visibility, consistency, engagement | Publishing private notes without packaging | Clear public artifacts, concise posts, repeatable themes |
| Corporate Culture | Low-friction fit, optimism, perceived safety | Sounding bitter or too abstract | Grounded language, serious but cooperative tone, constructive critique |

## Competing Signal Types

| Signal | Strength | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| Credentials | HR legibility and baseline vocabulary | Credential inflation |
| Prior titles | Risk reduction and role continuity | Circular gatekeeping |
| Portfolio artifacts | Evidence of judgment and work style | Uninspected archive |
| Referrals | Trust transfer | Access inequality |
| Social proof | Visibility and familiarity | Spectacle |
| Profile aesthetics | First-impression polish | Surface over substance |
| Positive language | Low-friction fit | Corporate unreality |
| Direct outreach | Human contact and targeting | Spam if weak |
| Interview performance | Live trust formation | Performance anxiety / uneven evaluation |

## Operating Rule

Do not merely become more competent.

Convert competence into trust-bearing evidence.

## Artifact Test

A strong artifact should answer at least three of these:

```text
[ ] What scenario is being investigated?
[ ] What evidence was collected?
[ ] What decision was made?
[ ] What uncertainty remains?
[ ] What false positives are possible?
[ ] What framework or technique does this map to?
[ ] What action should an analyst take next?
[ ] What would improve the detection, process, or control?
[ ] Who is the reviewer and what trust problem does this solve for them?
[ ] How does this artifact become visible to the right person?
```
